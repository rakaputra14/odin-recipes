Spaghetti

Spaghetti is a long, slender pasta made from durum wheat, prized for its firm al dente texture and smooth surface that carries sauces evenly. Its elegant length and balanced chew make it a classic foundation for both light, oil-based preparations and richer, robust sauces, allowing flavors to cling while maintaining structure and bite.

Ingredients

  • Dried spaghetti (durum wheat semolina pasta)
  • Water
  • Salt
  • Olive oil
  • Garlic
  • Onion
  • Tomato sauce or crushed tomatoes
  • Ground beef or other meat (such as pork, chicken, or sausage)
  • Salt and black pepper
  • Herbs (such as basil or oregano)
  • Grated cheese (such as Parmesan)

Steps

  1. Bring salted water to a rolling boil
  2. Cook the spaghetti until al dente, then drain
  3. Heat olive oil in a pan and saut√© garlic and onion until fragrant
  4. Add meat and cook until browned
  5. Stir in tomato sauce and season with salt, pepper, and herbs
  6. Simmer the sauce until flavors develop
  7. Combine the cooked spaghetti with the sauce
  8. Plate the spaghetti and finish with grated cheese and optional garnish
